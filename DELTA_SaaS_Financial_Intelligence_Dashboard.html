AutoPilotAI — persistent sector underperformance · Confidence 71%

GridSecure — EBITDA swings 26pp in 12 months · Confidence 66%
EBITDA margin moved from −11.6% (Q4 2022) to +14.7% (Q3 2023) — a 26pp swing with no corresponding revenue inflection. Secondary hypothesis: variable cost structure tied to specific contract delivery milestones. 1 — Trigger S&M efficiency intervention across 6 underperforming companies
89% confidence
Portfolio-wide S&M intensity averages 40.3% of ARR — 8–12pp above SaaS benchmarks for comparable growth profiles (OpenView SaaS Benchmarks 2024). Negative operating leverage is systemic: S&M is not converting efficiently into net new ARR despite burn multiples of 0.01× indicating efficient cash deployment. The paradox — efficient cash use but poor S&M-to-growth conversion — signals structural GTM execution failure, not capital scarcity.

Targets: CloudNest (43.5%), NexaFlow (42.8%), PayStream (42.0%), DataPulse (40.6%), MediTrack (41.4%), EduSphere (40.2%). Implement zero-based S&M budget audit in Q3 2026. Target reduction from 40% → 33% of ARR, releasing 7pp to EBITDA across cohort.

Quantified impact: At $2.12T combined ARR, a 7pp S&M reduction generates approximately $148B in annualized EBITDA improvement. At sector P/S multiples of 8–12×, this represents $1.2–1.8T in enterprise value creation. Expected Rule of 40 improvement: +4–6pp across cohort within 2 quarters.
2 — Reclassify EduSphere, OmniDesk, LegalEdge as strategic optionality assets
78% confidence
Three companies ($1.1M–$8.3M ARR) are below the minimum viable SaaS scale threshold for organic profitability at current growth rates. NRR >100% confirms product-market fit but the S&M engine is insufficient to accelerate growth to escape velocity. These are classic "wedge" SaaS products — genuine value creators that cannot independently scale to profitability without strategic partnership or capital injection.

Board decision framework within 2 quarters: (a) M&A exit — all three are natural bolt-on targets for vertical SaaS consolidators; LegalEdge fits LegalTech platforms, EduSphere fits EdTech acquirers, OmniDesk fits CX/support suite consolidators. At 8–10× ARR multiples, valuations of $9M–$66M are achievable. (b) Market adjacency pivot — fresh Series A/B capital to fund 18-month growth acceleration with EBITDA covenant. (c) Hold with monitoring — only viable if combined operating cost is below $500K/month and runway exceeds 24 months.

Confidence in organic recovery without intervention: 22%.

83% confidence

Best-in-class SaaS companies drive 40–50% net new ARR from expansion at Series C+ (Bessemer Cloud Index 2024). With NRR already >100% portfolio-wide and LTV/CAC ratios of 2,000–16,000×, the unit economic infrastructure for expansion revenue is in place; only the GTM playbook is absent.

The opportunity is largest at AutoPilotAI (27% → 40% = 13pp shift on $7.25B avg monthly net new MRR = +$942M/month incremental expansion) and NexaFlow (32% → 40% = 8pp shift on $8.4M avg monthly = +$672K/month). Portfolio-wide, a 5pp improvement in expansion contribution would add approximately $2.3B in annualized net new ARR without incremental CAC.

Mechanism: product-led expansion triggers, consumption-based pricing tiers, quarterly business reviews tied to upsell sequences, automated health-score-based expansion alerts.
